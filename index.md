Hello! I'm Iyer.

I'm an engineer at ParsecAerospace, where we're reinventing engines for aviation. On the scientific side, I'm working with fuels and materials, selecting (or creating) the right kinds for our requirements, so I'm pretty good at coming up with new ideas and solutions. On the technical side, I'm handling part manufacturing and engineering documentation, and that's made me a real stickler for following rules and processes properly.  

I graduated from BITS Pilani in July 2025 with a B.E., majoring in Chemical Engineering and minoring in Physics. I really enjoyed taking science courses, and ended up completing several in physics, chemistry, and biology. These courses were pleasant changes of pace from the engineering monotony, and I learnt a fair bit too. It was also great fun exercising free will and satisfying my curiosities.

I'm interested in all kinds of research, but particularly in chemical and materials science and engineering. In my free time I play around with open-source programs and applications in these areas — something I've been doing since my undergraduate days. It follows that my research experience is mostly computational. As a consequence of jumping from one research field to another in my undergraduate days to see what stuck, I'm also _really_ good at literature reviews, and several few-week surveys I engaged in have ended up under review or published. 

Outside of scientific and academic pursuits, I like quizzing and sports, both watching and participating in them. I'm also keeping myself busy learning Japanese.

On this website you can view or download my [CV](https://adityahiyer.github.io/cv), articles I've co-authored, and some other projects I've worked on. I also like to write about Bengaluru's metro system, and you can find my thoughts here. Thanks for visiting!

Currently based in Bengaluru, India.
